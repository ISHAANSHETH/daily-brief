Zerodha Eyes Investment Banking Licence

Zerodha has applied to SEBI for a Category I merchant banking licence, marking its first formal move into investment banking. The application comes against the backdrop of India's strong IPO pipeline and expanding capital markets activity. If approved, Zerodha would be able to advise on and manage public offerings, significantly broadening its financial services footprint beyond retail broking.

Amazon Triggers $15 Billion Quick-Commerce Rout

Shares of Eternal and Swiggy have suffered a combined $15 billion rout as Amazon and Flipkart ramp up their push into India's 10-minute delivery segment. Blinkit and Instamart, the quick-commerce arms of Eternal and Swiggy respectively, have seen their valuations squeezed sharply amid intensifying competitive pressure. The sell-off underscores how quickly incumbents can be repriced when deep-pocketed rivals enter a high-growth market.

PayU Swings to $18M Ebitda Profit

Prosus-owned fintech firm PayU swung to an $18 million full-year Ebitda profit in FY26, reversing a prior loss position. The turnaround was driven by a deliberate shift toward higher-margin products and ecosystem lending, while the company exited lower-value UPI categories. The result signals a maturing credit and payments business as PayU sharpens its focus on profitability over volume.

Persistent Plunges 11% on Nagarro Deal

Persistent Systems' share price fell 11% after the IT company announced the acquisition of Munich-based Nagarro SE, its largest-ever deal, at an enterprise valuation of €1.27 billion. Investors appear concerned about the scale of the outlay and the execution risks associated with integrating a major European technology firm. The sharp reaction highlights the market's sensitivity to large cross-border acquisitions by Indian mid-cap IT players.

India Plans Informal-Economy Tracker for RBI

India is developing a high-frequency index under MoSPI to track its vast informal economy, covering millions of unincorporated enterprises. The proposed index is designed to improve GDP estimates, guide RBI interest-rate decisions, and give corporates and investors a more accurate real-time read on economic activity. The initiative would address a longstanding blind spot in India's macroeconomic data architecture.

GIP-Backed Pristine Logistics Files Confidential IPO

Pristine Logistics, backed by Global Infrastructure Partners, has taken the confidential filing route for its upcoming IPO, appointing Axis, CLSA, and SBI as book-running managers. The listing fits a broader pattern of infrastructure asset monetisation in India, where the government's record ₹12.2 trillion capital expenditure budget is drawing sustained interest from global private equity. The confidential route allows the company to gauge investor appetite before committing to a public filing timeline.
