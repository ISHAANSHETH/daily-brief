✍ Editor's Take

Nifty slipping 0.46% with a PCR of 0.772 is a market that's leaning bearish on the surface but nowhere near panic — VIX at 13.61 is still well-behaved, so don't let the dip spook you into selling good positions. What's actually telling here is the DII net buying of nearly ₹5,748 crore against a token FII inflow of ₹384 crore — domestic institutions are using every soft day to accumulate, and that's your real floor signal. Auto down over 2% is a red flag worth watching; if that weakness persists beyond a couple of sessions, it stops being noise and starts being a theme, so keep your stop-losses tight on auto names. Pharma holding up with over 1% gain while IT bleeds again confirms a rotation that's been quietly building — if you're still overweight IT hoping for a mean reversion, you're fighting the tape. Pankaj Pandey's call on banks, power finance, and infra aligns with where the smart domestic money appears to be moving, and that's where you want your next rupee deployed.

Regulatory Watch — SEBI · RBI

Zerodha has applied to SEBI for a Category I merchant banking licence, a move that would allow India's largest retail broker to underwrite IPOs, manage QIPs, and advise on M&A — functions currently dominated by ICICI Securities, Kotak, and Axis Capital (Source: ET Markets). If approved, it signals broker-to-full-stack-financial-services consolidation is accelerating. Separately, India is designing a high-frequency index to track the informal economy — millions of unincorporated enterprises currently invisible to RBI's monetary policy models (Source: ET Economy). Better informal-sector data could sharpen rate decisions, but also raises the risk of policy overcorrection if the index methodology is flawed at launch.
