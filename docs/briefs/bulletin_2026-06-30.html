Oyo Parent Files ₹6,650 Crore IPO

Prism, the parent company of Oyo, has filed an updated DRHP for a ₹6,650 crore IPO, with the bulk of proceeds earmarked for debt repayment and the remainder for general corporate purposes. The company reported a ₹748 crore net profit for the nine months ending December 31, 2025, a significant turnaround from prior losses, though ₹559 crore of that figure was driven by a deferred-tax benefit. India now accounts for less than 12% of Oyo's revenues, with the US emerging as its largest market. The filing also includes plans for a potential pre-IPO placement.

Fibe Eyes ₹2,000 Crore IPO

TPG-backed fintech lender Fibe plans to file for an IPO of up to ₹2,000 crore by July, while its parent entity Social Worth Technologies has already filed a DRHP with SEBI to raise up to ₹750 crore. Founded in 2015, Social Worth Technologies operates the EarlySalary Services subsidiary, and proceeds will support that unit alongside broader corporate needs. The dual filing activity signals strong momentum in the fintech IPO pipeline heading into the second half of 2025.

SMBC Injects $12–15 Million Into Fintechs

SMBC Asia Rising Fund has made follow-on investments totalling $12–15 million across three fintech startups: Easy Home Finance, Vayana, and DPDzero. The move underscores ARF's sustained conviction in India's fintech sector and the growth opportunities within housing finance, trade financing, and collections technology. Separately, South Korea's government fund KVIC is also courting Indian VC firms, signalling a broader Asian institutional push into India's deeptech and advanced manufacturing sectors.

PayU Turns Ebitda Profitable in FY26

Prosus-owned fintech firm PayU swung to an $18 million full-year Ebitda profit in FY26, driven by higher-margin products and ecosystem lending. The company also exited lower-value UPI categories as part of a deliberate strategic shift toward more profitable business lines. The turnaround marks a significant milestone for PayU as it repositions its payments and credit businesses for sustainable growth.

Amazon Rout Wipes $15 Billion From Eternal, Swiggy

Amazon's aggressive rapid-delivery push has triggered a combined $15 billion market-value rout across Eternal and Swiggy, rattling India's quick-commerce duopoly. The selloff reflects investor concern that Amazon's entry could materially erode the market share and unit economics of the two incumbents. The development adds competitive pressure to a sector already navigating high fulfilment costs and thin margins.

Binance AI Blocks $10.5 Billion in Fraud

Binance disclosed that its AI-powered compliance systems intercepted $10.53 billion in potential fraud between 2025 and the first quarter of 2026, as the exchange spends approximately $300 million annually on compliance infrastructure. The figures highlight the scale of illicit activity targeting crypto platforms and the growing role of machine-learning tools in financial crime prevention. The disclosure comes as regulators globally intensify scrutiny of digital-asset exchanges.
