RBI Flags NBFC Stress, AI Cyber Risk

India's RBI warned in its latest report that NBFC stressed assets could hit 2.8% by March 2027, with capital buffers dipping to 20.8% under normal conditions and deteriorating further under severe stress scenarios. Separately, the central bank identified AI-powered cyberattacks as the single biggest near-term threat to India's financial system, noting that preparedness across banks and NBFCs remains uneven. The RBI also flagged rising third-party dependencies as a compounding vulnerability, while asserting that the broader banking system remains resilient with strong capital and healthy profits.

Oyo Parent Files ₹6,650 Crore IPO

Oyo's parent company Prism has filed an updated DRHP with SEBI for a ₹6,650 crore IPO, with the bulk of proceeds earmarked for debt repayment and the remainder for general corporate purposes, including a potential pre-IPO placement. The company reported a ₹748 crore net profit for the nine months ending December 31, 2025 — a significant turnaround from prior losses — though ₹559 crore of that figure was driven by a deferred-tax benefit, raising questions about underlying earnings quality. India now accounts for less than 12% of Oyo's revenues, with the United States having grown into its largest market.

PayU Swings to $18M Ebitda Profit

Prosus-owned fintech firm PayU swung to an $18-million full-year Ebitda profit in FY26, driven by higher-margin products and ecosystem lending. The company also exited lower-value UPI categories as part of a deliberate shift toward more profitable business lines. The turnaround marks a meaningful inflection point for one of India's largest payments and credit platforms.

SMBC Injects $12–15M Into Three Fintechs

SMBC Asia Rising Fund has made follow-on investments totaling $12–15 million across three Indian fintech startups: Easy Home Finance, Vayana, and DPDzero. The investments signal continued institutional confidence in India's fintech lending and B2B payments infrastructure. The fund's repeat bets underscore a broader trend of Asian institutional capital deepening its commitment to Indian financial technology.

Fibe Parent Files ₹750 Crore IPO

Social Worth Technologies Ltd, parent of consumer lending app Fibe, has filed a DRHP with SEBI to raise up to ₹750 crore through an IPO. Proceeds will be deployed to support its subsidiary EarlySalary Services and meet general corporate needs. Founded in 2015, the company has diversified its product suite beyond its salary-advance roots ahead of the public market debut.

ESMA Recognises India Clearing Corporation

The European Securities and Markets Authority has granted formal recognition to India's Clearing Corporation, ending a prolonged regulatory dispute. The landmark approval allows the Clearing Corporation of India to operate within European market frameworks, opening cross-border clearing pathways. The move is expected to ease access for foreign participants transacting in Indian debt and derivatives markets.
