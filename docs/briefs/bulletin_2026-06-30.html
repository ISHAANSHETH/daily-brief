Oyo Parent Files ₹6,650 Crore IPO

Prism, formerly Oravel Stays and the parent of Oyo, has filed an updated DRHP for a ₹6,650 crore IPO that includes a potential pre-IPO placement. The bulk of the proceeds will be used to repay borrowings, with the remainder earmarked for general corporate purposes. The company reported a ₹748 crore net profit for the nine months ending December 31, 2025, though analysts note that figure was boosted by a ₹559 crore deferred-tax credit. India now accounts for less than 12% of Oyo's revenues, with the US emerging as its largest market.

Fibe Eyes Up to ₹2,000 Crore IPO

TPG-backed fintech lender Fibe plans to file for an IPO of up to ₹2,000 crore by July. Its parent, Social Worth Technologies Ltd, has already filed a DRHP with SEBI to raise up to ₹750 crore, with proceeds earmarked to support subsidiary EarlySalary Services and corporate needs. Founded in 2015, Fibe has diversified its product suite beyond salary advances into broader consumer credit. The dual filing activity signals strong appetite for fintech listings in the current market cycle.

SMBC ARF Backs Three Indian Fintechs

SMBC Asia Rising Fund has made follow-on investments of $12–15 million across three Indian fintech startups: Easy Home Finance, Vayana, and DPDzero. The capital injection underscores the fund's continued conviction in India's fintech infrastructure and lending ecosystem. This marks a deepening of SMBC's strategic commitment to the Indian market beyond initial bets.

PayU Swings to $18M Ebitda Profit

Prosus-owned fintech firm PayU swung to an $18 million full-year Ebitda profit in FY26, driven by higher-margin products and ecosystem lending. The turnaround came as the company deliberately exited lower-value UPI categories to sharpen its margin profile. The results highlight a broader strategic pivot toward credit and premium payment services.

Amazon Rout Wipes $15 Billion From Eternal, Swiggy

Amazon's aggressive push into rapid delivery has triggered a combined $15 billion market-cap rout for Eternal and Swiggy. The development signals intensifying competitive pressure on India's established quick-commerce players from the global e-commerce giant. Investors are reassessing the long-term margin and market-share assumptions underpinning both companies' valuations.

South Korea's KVIC Courts Indian VC Firms

South Korea's government-backed KVIC and other Korean institutional investors are actively scouting partnerships with Indian venture capital firms, signaling a structured strategic shift toward India's deeptech and advanced manufacturing sectors. The move reflects evolving allocation strategies among Korean LPs who see India as a priority emerging-market destination. This cross-border LP interest could open new funding channels for Indian startups beyond traditional US and domestic sources.
