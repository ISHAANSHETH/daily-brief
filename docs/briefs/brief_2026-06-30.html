✍ Editor's Take

Nifty's mild pullback to 23865 is not the story — the story is IT bleeding out 2.73% while Metal and Realty quietly held the fort, and that tells you exactly where institutional conviction is sitting right now. FIIs pulled out ₹1350 crore but DIIs absorbed more than double that at ₹2801 crore, which means domestic money is not panicking — it's actually buying the dip selectively, and you should pay attention to what they're likely accumulating. The RBI stress test flagging NBFC capital buffers thinning by FY27 is the kind of slow-burn warning that markets dismiss today and reprice violently later — trim your frothy NBFC exposure now while the VIX at 13.5 keeps premiums cheap enough to hedge. Auto's 1.85% fall deserves a hard look: between margin pressure, rural demand uncertainty, and global slowdown fears, this sector is not a "buy the dip" candidate yet. The RBI's AI-cyberattack warning is not boilerplate — financial infrastructure risk is real and rising, so fintech-heavy portfolios need a second look at their risk-reward before the next earnings cycle arrives.

Macro View BEARISH

USD/INR sits at 84.66 — wait, the provided rate is 94.66, which if accurate would represent a historic rupee collapse; flagging this as a number to verify before publishing. US 10Y yields hold at 4.4%, keeping dollar-denominated capital expensive and EM outflows a live risk for Indian debt markets. RBI's latest report names AI-enabled cyberattacks the single biggest near-term threat to India's financial system, with banks and NBFCs most exposed (Source: ET Industry). The same stress cycle is already visible in NBFC balance sheets — RBI's stress tests flag rising bad loans with capital buffers projected to thin materially by FY27 (Source: ET Industry). Thinner capital cushions mean tighter credit to the real economy precisely when consumption needs support. Bear case: a credit event in one large NBFC could transmit stress system-wide faster than regulators can respond.
